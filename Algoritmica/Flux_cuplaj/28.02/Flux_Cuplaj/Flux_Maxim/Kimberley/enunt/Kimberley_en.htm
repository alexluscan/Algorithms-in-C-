kimberley

The history of Kimberley City begins with the first diamond discovered in South Africa, east of Orange river, in 1867.
The first houses built in this place by diamond diggers are now in the center of the city.
Between 1870-1880, the Kimberley mine was producing more than 95% of the total number of diamonds in the entire world. This is where the famous "Star of Africa" diamond was found.
The hill that was the initial dig is now gone, and there is a huge pit where it stood.

Let's go back to 1870, back when the mine was made up of a series of underground tunnels.
Our problem supposes that Kimberley mine is made up of G very, very narrow tunnels. The time needed by a miner to go through any of the tunnels is of one minute. During each minute, only a single miner may cross a tunnel, either one way or the other.
Work areas are called "rooms". Each room is the end of one or more tunnels and each tunnel has at its two ends a room. Any two rooms communicate via one or more tunnels that unite them directly.
There are N rooms in Kimberley mine, which the miners identify via numbers 1, 2, ... N. We suppose that a team made up of M miners finished working the night shift. They all worked in work area number S. They have to reach room D, which is located at the mine's exit. There is always a risk of tunnel cave-ins. For this reason, the miner team wants to exit the mine in the shortest amount of time possible.

Task

Find the minimum required amount of time so that all miners from the night shift may exit the mine.

Input Data

File kimberley.in has on line one positive integers N and G, on line two three positive integers S, D and M, and on the following G lines, two positive integers representing the number of the rooms at the end of each of the G tunnels. The numbers from each line are separated by a space.

Output Data

File kimberley.out will contain a single line with number T, representing the shortest amount of time in which all miners will reach room D, the Kimberley mine exit.

Restrictions and Mentions

  • 1 <= N, S, D <= 50, 1 <= G <= 100, 1 <= M <= 150
  • No tunnel has the same room at both ends.
  • Any two room are connected by no more than one tunnel.
  • Any room is sufficiently large to host all the miners at the same time.
  • The only persons in the mine are the N miners from the night shift.

Example

In minute five, miner 3 goes to room 1 .

Time limit: 0.15 seconds
Total available memory: 7 Mb, of which 1 Mb for the stack.
